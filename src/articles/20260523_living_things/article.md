## Fermentation or something
Healthy for you, they say, easy, they say, makes you grow to more than 2 meters tall, they say. 
Summer is the time to try alchemy again. 
I follow more or less this [how to make kombucha recipe](https://brewbuch.com/how-to-make-kombucha/).
Seems easy, you build a scoby, feed it tea, collect kombucha, put it in bottles, make sure they don't explode, profit. 

## Log of events
### Setup
The hardest thing when growing your own scoby seems buying the "right" kombucha.
- Non pasteurized
- Non flavoured
I found a bottle of greentea-kombucha that might be suitable in a store selling organic goods. 

Next quest: Finding big enough jars to build a home-kombucha factory. While this seemed tricky at first, I found 3-4 suitable ones in one supermarket. Living in Switzerland? I suggest you go to Coop. I went for one with a little spout to better fill bottles with the strategy to use it as a water dispenser at parties should everything go wrong. 

### 2026-03-23 Building a scoby, first try
After boiling water, already steeping tee in sweet water, I destroyed the big jar during cleaning with hot water.
Sadly, shops are already closed over the long weekend. Time to make ice-tea. Or just using a smaller 1l glass from the basement. I hope the scoby does not need "enough" tea, even though I believe that less added kombucha might reduce the probability of success. Let's see how this develops.

### 2026-06-24 Update
No Scobie grow - the tea developed a layer, but it's improbable, that this is a scoby. Based on the instructions, a scobie should be present. This might either be related to the base drink (green tea is not recommendet for growing a scobie), to not having enough black tea, or to something completely different, like heat, chance and so on.
