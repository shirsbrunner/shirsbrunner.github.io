Life, Work and Projects

I just visited Japan again for a three week trip with the family. The contrasts between all the high tech on one hand and natural or spiritual things on the other hand still impresses me. The same is true for the big companies in shiny skyscrappers next to the small udon shops in the back alleys. It's as if there are two different countries occupying the same space. I love this contrast between large and modern things but also the dedication for small and sometimes deliberately imperfect things. I also like cherry blossoms.

I work for we are cube³, the UX-division of Puzzle ITC on multiple different projects. In the last half year, I've been pushing a lot of energy into projects and also work or organization related discussions. In hindsight, the latter took up a lot of space in my mind – and also absorbed a lot of creative energy. I eventually got the growing feeling that I haven't made enough small things for a long time. Thus, in the evenings, I currently feel like adding little details to this page or improving existing things.

Besides work and this page, I made some posters and flyers for two exhibitions. Currently, I'm working on a new typeface with lots of dark pixels that I hope to use on the page eventually. Furthermore I have some ideas for stupid webapplications. Let's see, what I can do.

Whut?

I'm glad you ask. This is a now page, and if you have your own site, maybe you should make one, too?

This feels like a small thing to do, but maybe it isn't. I read a lot of people having problems updating now pages. Maybe you just don't feel like sharing some things with the world? Anyway, I'll give it a try and see what happens.
